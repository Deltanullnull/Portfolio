About me

I was but four years old when I came in contact with my first computer - in a time where Windows 3.1 was the latest installment of a series of graphical operating systems, created with the purpose to facilitate user interaction compared to the (for many users) more unappealing MS-DOS console. This however did not prevent my younger self to hover his fingers over the keyboard to type in the most bizarre commands.

From there on forward, my future career has been more or less decided. I joined my first programming activity at the age of 13 and later took part in regular classes during the final years of high school. During my time at university I was able to extend my knowledge considerably and developed a particular interest in computer graphics and computer vision. That interest influenced me in my choice of my graduation thesis about 3D scanning and motion capture. This thesis also served as the topic of my research project in Hiroshima.

The skills acquired during my studies helped me to quickly land a job as a Mixed Reality Developer in Japan. Not only have I become an integral part in building up a multicultural environment in a small-sized company but also was able to contribute to the planning and development of projects in cooperation with top-tier Japanese companies, such as Sony Semiconductor Solutions etc.
